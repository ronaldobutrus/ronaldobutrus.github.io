Work Experience

Football Referee
June 2019 - Present

In Summer 2019 I undertook the FA's introductory referee course, which featured a combined forty hours of training and observation to become an FA-Qualified Referee.

A few months later, I became eligible for a promotion to a Level 7 Referee, thus allowing me to officiate in both adult and youth amateur football leagues. This qualification is internationally recognised by FIFA and respective National FAs.
Currently, I am officiating with Harrow Youth Football League (HYFL) and Middlesex FA.

Refereeing has led me to develop the following skills:
- Collaboration: refereeing requires constant collaboration both on and off the field of play. From communicating with secretaries to secure match details to dealing with on-field confrontations, collaboration is key to officiation.
- Decision-making: as a referee, I am required to make difficult decisions around the clock. This ranges from deciding whether a pitch is playable to whether or not a player is guilty of a sending-off offence. Furthermore, decisions made as a referee will almost always incite disagreement from either parties; hence I am obliged to present suitable degree of self-confidence throughout my role.
- Communication: it's all about communication. Throughout any match, I have a duty to both give and receive signals to assistant referees and the fourth official and simultaneously make these signals as discreet as possible to avoid reactions from players, substitutes and team officials.
